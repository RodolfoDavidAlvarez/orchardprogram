1. PURPOSE OF THE DOCUMENT

This document provides Soil Seed & Water representatives with a clear, standardized roadmap for targeting, engaging, selling, implementing, and supporting specialty orchard/vineyard clients in the Business-to-Business (Specialty Farmer) Market Segment.

This playbook establishes the complete outreach-to-fulfillment system, simplifies field execution, and aligns all teams on how to deliver regenerative orchard programs using Pomona and SSW protocols.

KEY OBJECTIVES:

  • Standardize the sales approach across all representatives
  • Provide clear, actionable steps from lead generation to customer success
  • Integrate CRM workflows at every stage of the sales process
  • Establish measurable KPIs and timelines for program execution
  • Create a repeatable system for scaling orchard/vineyard client acquisition

WHO THIS DOCUMENT IS FOR:

  • Sales representatives targeting specialty farmers
  • Field representatives conducting site visits
  • Marketing team developing campaigns and content
  • Management tracking performance and pipeline metrics
  • Operations team coordinating fulfillment and delivery

HOW TO USE THIS PLAYBOOK:

This document is designed as a step-by-step execution guide. Each section builds upon the previous one, creating a complete sales and implementation system. Use the table of contents to navigate directly to specific sections as needed, but we recommend reviewing the entire document to understand the full process flow.

2. MARKETING/SALES PIPELINE OVERVIEW

The marketing and sales pipeline is the backbone of our orchard program execution. It provides a clear, visual framework that guides every interaction from initial lead identification through ongoing account management. Each stage is designed to move prospects closer to becoming long-term SSW clients.

VISUAL PIPELINE FLOW

Each stage feeds into the next, with CRM tracking and automation at every step. This ensures no prospect falls through the cracks and every interaction is documented and optimized.

PIPELINE STAGES EXPLAINED

1. Lead List

Comprehensive database of all specialty orchard/vineyard growers within our 300-mile target radius. Includes complete profiles: name, farm name, location, crop type, acreage, contact information, and water source. All leads are entered and tagged in CRM by December 15, 2024.

2. Initial Outreach (Hook Point)

First-touch message designed to capture attention and create interest. Launched January 1, 2025, this multi-channel campaign (email/text/call) introduces SSW and our regenerative approach. Hook Point messages are personalized where possible and loaded into CRM as automated outbound campaigns.

3. Nurture (Value Content)

Educational content sequence that builds credibility and trust. Beginning January 15, 2025, short value messages or videos are deployed via CRM as scheduled drip content. This stage follows an Education â†’ Credibility â†’ Proposal flow, progressively delivering value while preparing prospects for a sales conversation.

4. Smart Calling

Targeted phone outreach beginning February 1, 2025. Call lists are pulled from CRM based on engagement metrics (opens, clicks, replies). Representatives prioritize prospects showing the highest engagement, ensuring efficient use of time and resources. All call outcomes are logged in CRM with notes, objections, and next steps.

5. Site Visits

Monthly field visits scheduled through CRM calendar integration. One week each month is dedicated to on-farm site visits. During visits, representatives document soil issues, take photos, record conversations, and make product recommendationsâ€”all directly in CRM records. Visit reports are generated automatically.

6. Trials/Samples

9 lb trial bags sent to qualified leads, along with branded candies. Sample shipments are entered into CRM as activities with automated follow-up reminders. Response and engagement are tracked to identify trial conversion opportunities.

7. Ongoing Accounts

Established customers receiving regular Pomona applications and SSW protocol support. These accounts move through implementation, monitoring, and seasonal review cycles. CRM tracks application schedules, field observations, and renewal discussions.

CRM INTEGRATION AT EVERY STAGE

Every stage of the pipeline is fully integrated with our CRM system:

  • Lead Capture: All prospects entered with complete profiles
  • Segmentation: Tagged by crop type, region, priority, acreage, water source, and buying potential
  • Automation: Hook Point campaigns and value drips scheduled and tracked
  • Tracking: Open/click rates, response rates, and activity timelines monitored weekly
  • Workflow: Smart calling lists, site visit scheduling, and sample tracking all managed in CRM
  • Pipeline Management: Prospects move through stages: Lead â†’ Engaged â†’ Visit â†’ Trial â†’ Customer
  • Reporting: Weekly dashboards showing leads, conversions, response rates, and projected revenue

KEY SUCCESS METRICS

  • Lead generation targets by crop type and region
  • Hook Point campaign open and response rates
  • Value content engagement metrics
  • Smart calling conversion rates
  • Site visit to trial conversion percentage
  • Trial to customer conversion rate
  • Customer retention and renewal rates

This pipeline structure ensures a systematic, scalable approach to orchard client acquisition while maintaining personalization and relationship-building at every stage.
