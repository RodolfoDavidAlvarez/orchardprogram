Orchards Program - Business-to-Business (Specialty Farmer) Market Segment
Execution Playbook

============================================================================
PURPOSE OF THE DOCUMENT
============================================================================
Provide Soil Seed & Water representatives with a clear, standardized roadmap 
for targeting, engaging, selling, implementing, and supporting specialty 
orchard/vineyard clients in the Business-to-Business (Specialty Farmer) 
Market Segment.

This document establishes the full outreach-to-fulfillment system, simplifies 
field execution, and aligns all teams on how to deliver regenerative orchard 
programs using Pomona and SSW protocols.

4. OUTREACH STRATEGY & TACTICS
============================================================================

PROSPECT IDENTIFICATION:

RESEARCH METHODS & DATA SOURCES:
• Agricultural county records and crop reports
• USDA agricultural census data
• State agricultural department databases
• Crop insurance records (where accessible)
• Agricultural extension office directories
• Trade association member lists (pistachio, citrus, wine grape associations)
• Tradeshow attendee lists and exhibitor directories
• Agricultural publication subscriber lists
• LinkedIn and professional network searches
• Google Maps satellite imagery for orchard identification
• Local agricultural supply dealer referrals
• Existing customer referrals and introductions
• Agricultural consultant/PCA networks

GEOGRAPHIC MAPPING PROCESS:
• Map all target orchards within 300-mile radius from SSW operations
• Create priority zones based on crop density and market value
• Identify clusters by crop type (pistachio regions, citrus belts, wine country)
• Flag irrigation water sources (district, well, surface water)
• Note access and logistics constraints (road conditions, delivery points)
• Document proximity to existing SSW customers for referral opportunities
• Create visual heatmaps by county/region for strategic planning

CONTACT INFORMATION GATHERING:
• Farm name and legal entity name
• Primary contact: owner name, title, phone, email
• Secondary contacts: farm manager, operations manager
• Technical contacts: PCA, CCA, agricultural consultant
• Mailing address and physical farm location (GPS coordinates if possible)
• Preferred communication method (phone, email, text)
• Best time to contact
• Social media profiles (LinkedIn, Facebook business pages)

DATABASE STRUCTURE & REQUIRED FIELDS:
• Basic Information: Farm name, contact name, title, phone, email, address
• Crop Details: Crop type(s), total acreage, orchard age, block layout
• Geographic: County, region, GPS coordinates, distance from SSW operations
• Water & Infrastructure: Water source type, irrigation method, equipment available
• Current Practices: Fertilizer program, soil management approach, input costs (if known)
• Pain Points: Primary challenges (soil health, water, yield, costs)
• Business Context: Decision-maker role, buying timeline, budget indicators
• Engagement Status: Initial contact date, last interaction, response level
• Priority Level: High/medium/low based on acreage, crop value, fit, accessibility
• Tags: Crop type tags, region tags, engagement tags, source tags

PRIORITIZATION CRITERIA:
• High Priority: Large acreage (100+ acres), high-value crops, within 200 miles, clear pain points, decision-maker identified
• Medium Priority: Medium acreage (50-100 acres), good crop fit, 200-300 mile radius, some engagement indicators
• Low Priority: Smaller operations, longer distance, unclear fit, limited contact information

DATABASE BUILDING & MAINTENANCE:
• Entry deadline: December 15, 2024 - all target prospects entered into CRM
• Weekly updates: Add new prospects as discovered, update contact information
• Monthly review: Verify data accuracy, remove duplicates, update engagement status
• Quarterly refresh: Reassess priority levels, update acreage/crop information, refresh contact details
• Data hygiene: Regular cleanup of bounced emails, disconnected phones, inactive contacts
• Source tracking: Tag each prospect with original source for ROI analysis

INITIAL OUTREACH - HOOK POINT (Jan 1, 2025):

HOOK POINT MESSAGE OBJECTIVES:
• Capture attention in first 10 seconds
• Establish relevance to their specific crop/challenge
• Create curiosity without overselling
• Invite engagement (response, click, call)
• Position SSW as knowledgeable partner, not vendor

MESSAGE CHANNELS:
• Email: Primary channel for initial outreach, allows tracking
• Text/SMS: For prospects who prefer text or have limited email access
• Phone: Warm calls to highest priority prospects, referral introductions
• LinkedIn: Professional network outreach for decision-makers
• Multi-channel: Combine email + LinkedIn for maximum visibility

PERSONALIZATION REQUIREMENTS:
• Use farm name and contact name
• Reference specific crop type in opening
• Mention geographic location or region
• Reference common challenge for their crop type
• Avoid generic mass messaging
• Customize based on available data (acreage, known challenges)

HOOK POINT CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE:
• Subject line: Crop-specific, benefit-focused, curiosity-driven
• Opening line: Direct, relevant, attention-grabbing
• Value statement: Brief benefit specific to their situation
• Soft call-to-action: Invitation to learn more, not hard sell
• Signature: Name, title, company, contact information
• Examples: 1-2 sample messages included in Appendix

CAMPAIGN DEPLOYMENT:
• Load into CRM as automated outbound campaign
• Schedule for January 1, 2025 launch
• Set up automated tracking (opens, clicks, replies)
• Configure follow-up sequences based on engagement
• Monitor response rates and adjust messaging as needed

DISCOVERY QUESTIONS:

SOIL HEALTH ASSESSMENT:
• What is your current approach to soil health management?
• Have you done soil tests recently? What did they show?
• What is your current organic matter level? (if known)
• Are you seeing any soil compaction issues?
• How is your microbial activity? (if they test for it)
• What soil amendments are you currently using?

FERTILIZER & NUTRITION:
• What fertilizer program are you running currently?
• How much are you spending per acre on fertilizers annually?
• Are you seeing nutrient deficiencies in your trees/crops?
• How do you apply fertilizers? (ground application, foliar, fertigation)
• Have you tried reducing fertilizer inputs? What happened?
• What micronutrient challenges are you facing?

WATER MANAGEMENT:
• What is your water source? (district, well, surface)
• How efficient is your irrigation system?
• Are you facing any water restrictions or challenges?
• What is your water cost per acre-foot?
• Have you noticed water infiltration issues?
• How are you managing water during drought periods?

YIELD & PERFORMANCE:
• What are your yield goals for this season?
• How have yields been trending over the past 3-5 years?
• What are your biggest yield-limiting factors?
• Are you seeing quality issues (size, color, grade)?
• What is your target harvest timing?
• How do current yields compare to your best years?

REGENERATIVE PRACTICES:
• Have you tried any regenerative agriculture practices?
• What was your experience with those practices?
• Are you interested in reducing synthetic inputs?
• What barriers have prevented you from adopting regenerative practices?
• Do you have experience with biological products or compost teas?

BUDGET & DECISION-MAKING:
• Who makes the decisions about soil and fertility programs?
• What is your typical budget range for soil/fertility inputs?
• How do you evaluate new products or programs?
• What would need to happen for you to try something new?
• Are you planning any changes to your program this season?
• What is your decision-making timeline?

SMART CALLING FRAMEWORK (Feb 1, 2025):

CALL LIST GENERATION:
• Pull lists from CRM based on engagement metrics
• Prioritize by: opens, clicks, replies, website visits
• Sort by priority level (high/medium/low)
• Group by crop type for consistent messaging
• Schedule calls during optimal times (morning, early afternoon)
• Target 20-30 calls per day per representative

CALL SCRIPT STRUCTURE:
• Opening: Name, company, brief context (reference email if sent)
• Permission: "Do you have 2-3 minutes to talk?"
• Value hook: One specific benefit relevant to their crop/situation
• Discovery: 2-3 key questions to understand their situation
• Qualification: Assess fit, budget, timeline, decision-maker
• Next step: Propose specific follow-up (visit, sample, information)
• Closing: Confirm next step, set timeline, thank them
• Script templates available in Appendix

OBJECTION HANDLING:
• "We're not interested" → Acknowledge, ask what they're currently doing, offer value (information, not sale)
• "We don't have budget" → Understand their current spend, discuss ROI, offer trial/sample
• "We tried something like this before" → Ask what happened, differentiate SSW approach, offer case study
• "We're happy with our current program" → Acknowledge, ask about goals, offer to benchmark
• "Send me information" → Send specific relevant content, set follow-up call, don't just email and disappear
• "I need to think about it" → Set specific follow-up date, send additional value content, stay engaged

QUALIFICATION CRITERIA:
• Budget: Can they afford the program? (rough estimate based on acreage)
• Authority: Are they the decision-maker or can they influence the decision?
• Need: Do they have pain points we can solve?
• Timeline: When would they consider making a change?
• Fit: Is their operation suitable for our program? (crop, acreage, logistics)

CALL OUTCOMES & LOGGING:
• Log all call outcomes in CRM immediately after call
• Outcomes: Connected/Left voicemail/No answer/Busy/Wrong number
• Engagement level: Interested/Maybe/Not interested/Follow up needed
• Notes: Key objections, responses given, commitments made, next steps
• Follow-up tasks: Automatically create tasks based on call outcome
• Update pipeline stage: Move to "Engaged" if qualified interest shown

CALL SCHEDULING & FOLLOW-UP:
• Schedule follow-up calls based on prospect response and timeline
• Set reminders in CRM for promised callbacks
• Send value content after calls (case studies, articles, videos)
• Re-engage no-answers with different approach (email, text, LinkedIn)
• Track call frequency to avoid over-contacting

COMMUNICATION TEMPLATE - SEAN EXAMPLE:

SUCCESSFUL SEQUENCE STRUCTURE:
• Week 1: Initial Hook Point email (crop-specific, value-focused)
• Week 2: Follow-up email with case study or relevant content
• Week 3: LinkedIn connection request with personalized message
• Week 4: Value message (short video or article link)
• Week 5: Smart call (reference previous touchpoints)
• Week 6: Sample offer (if qualified and interested)
• Week 7: Site visit invitation (if sample sent or strong interest)

MULTI-CHANNEL APPROACH:
• Email: Primary channel for value delivery and education
• Phone: For relationship building and qualification
• LinkedIn: Professional networking and credibility building
• Text: For quick check-ins and scheduling
• In-person: Site visits for relationship deepening

LAYERED COMMUNICATION PRINCIPLES:
• Never send the same message twice
• Each touchpoint adds new value
• Reference previous interactions to show attention
• Vary the channel to increase visibility
• Build on previous conversations, don't repeat

CRM INTEGRATION:
• Document entire sequence in CRM as template
• Tag prospects with "Sean Example Sequence" for tracking
• Measure response rates and conversion for this approach
• Refine template based on results
• Make available to all representatives in Appendix
