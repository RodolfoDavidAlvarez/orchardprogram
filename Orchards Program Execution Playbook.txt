3. RESEARCH & ELIGIBILITY
================================================================================

Thorough research ensures we focus on the orchards, vineyards, and avocado 
operations most likely to benefit from our specialized crop blends and SSW 
protocols. Use these criteria to build a targeted list, qualify quickly, and 
assign priority before outreach begins.

3.1 Crop Focus (Expanded)

• Pistachios: High-value nut crop with strong ROI on soil health and 
  water-efficiency gains.
• Pome fruits (apples, pears): Focus on fruit quality, storability, and soil 
  structure improvements.
• Stone fruits (peach, plum, cherry, apricot, nectarine): Emphasize fruit set, 
  color, and consistency.
• Vineyards (wine/table/raisin grapes): Quality and yield stability through 
  improved nutrient uptake and soil biology.
• Avocados: Water efficiency, salinity management, and root health are primary 
  levers.
• Citrus (orange, lemon, lime, grapefruit): Peel quality, brix, and stress 
  mitigation in heat/drought.
• Other regional high-value orchards: Add local specialty crops as mapping 
  reveals clusters.

3.2 Geographic Scope

• 300-mile target radius from SSW operations, segmented by priority zones.
• Map by crop density and market value; create heatmaps of top counties/regions.
• Flag irrigation water source (district, well, surface) and any water restrictions.
• Note access/logistics: truck suitability, road conditions, and preferred 
  delivery points.

3.3 Common Crop Challenges

• Low organic matter and weak soil structure
• Compaction and limited infiltration
• Water inefficiency and uneven distribution
• Micronutrient deficiencies and poor nutrient uptake
• Low water-holding capacity
• Weed pressure and low microbial activity

3.4 Common Farmer Challenges

• Limited equipment access or downtime for applications
• Soil biology and nutrient management knowledge gaps
• Logistics constraints (labor, timing, trucking)
• Cost management pressures and aversion to input risk

3.5 Suitability Criteria

• Crop compatibility with appropriate blend (Pomona, Seriokai's Secret, or Bucchas) 
  + supporting amendments
• Economic viability (acreage, revenue potential, input cost baseline)
• Access and logistics feasibility (equipment fit, delivery access)
• Decision-maker identified and reachable (owner, farm manager, PCA/consultant)
• Clear agronomic pain points we can solve (water, nutrition, soil structure)

DATA TO CAPTURE IN CRM:

• Crop type, acreage, orchard age, and block layout
• Water source and irrigation method (drip, micro-sprinkler, flood)
• Current fertilizer/soil program and spend (if known)
• Soil test results (if available) - capture test data and analysis
• Water test results (if available) - water quality and composition data
• Orchard history - years in business, years of operation, historical context
• Pain points from the person - direct feedback on challenges, concerns, and needs
• Recent yield/quality trends
• Equipment available for application and delivery constraints
• Contact roles (owner, manager, PCA/CCAs) with phone/email preferences

4.1 What We Offer

We deliver long-term orchard, vineyard, and avocado health and regeneration 
through our specialized crop blends and supporting SSW protocols. This is not a 
single-product sale—it's a comprehensive regenerative soil health program 
designed to transform degraded soils into productive, resilient ecosystems.

CORE OFFERING:

• Regenerative soil health programs tailored to specialty orchards, vineyards, 
  and avocados
• Crop-specific blends: Pomona (orchards), Seriokai's Secret (avocados/citrus), 
  and Bucchas (vineyards)
• Supporting protocols: Compost blends, compost tea extracts (drench), clean wood 
  fiber, and mycorrhizal treatments
• Customized application schedules aligned with crop phenology and seasonal needs
• Ongoing field support, soil testing, and protocol adjustments
• Complete logistics coordination: delivery, equipment support, and application 
  guidance

SPECIALTY CROP BLENDS:

SSW offers three specialized crop blends designed for specific crop types and 
their unique nutritional needs. Each blend is OMRI-listed for organic use and 
formulated to address the distinct requirements of different specialty crops.

1. POMONA BLEND - For Orchards

   Primary Use: Stone fruits (peach, plum, cherry, apricot, nectarine), pome 
   fruits (apple, pear), pistachios, and other high-value orchard crops.
   
   Key Benefits: Promotes vigorous growth, abundant yields, and optimal fruit 
   quality through targeted micronutrient support and improved soil health.

2. SERIKAI'S SECRET BLEND - For Avocados and Citrus

   Primary Use: Avocados, citrus (oranges, lemons, limes, grapefruit), and 
   other subtropical tree crops.
   
   Key Benefits: Boosts root strength, fruit yield, and disease resistance while 
   improving soil structure and water retention.

3. BUCCHAS BLEND - For Vineyards

   Primary Use: Wine grapes, table grapes, raisin grapes, and other vine crops.
   
   Key Benefits: Supports grape vine health, fruit quality, and yield stability 
   through improved nutrient uptake and soil biology. Addresses the unique 
   nutritional requirements of grape production systems, including sugar 
   accumulation (brix) and overall vine health.

All blends are granular formulations that integrate with our comprehensive 
regenerative soil health protocols. Application rates and timing are customized 
based on crop type, soil conditions, and seasonal needs as part of our 
tailored program approach.

SUPPORTING PROTOCOL COMPONENTS:

4. Orchard/Vineyard Compost Blend

   • High-quality dairy compost applied under tree canopies
   • Builds organic matter, improves structure, creates microbial habitat
   • Year 1: 10 tons/canopy acre; maintenance: 5 tons/canopy acre annually

5. Compost Tea Extract (Drench)
   • Living microbial inoculant applied via water wagon or sprayer
   • Pre-bud break application (February–March) activates soil biology
   • Second application before nut set (June–July) supports nutrient cycling
   • Super cost-effective biological activation

6. Clean Wood Fiber
   • Mulch application between orchard rows
   • Erosion control, moisture retention, temperature regulation, weed suppression
   • Improves water infiltration by 50% and prevents topsoil loss

7. Mycorrhizal Backfill Mix
   • Specialized fungal blend for new plantings and tree replacement
   • One-time application establishes drought-tolerant root networks
   • 10x more cost-effective than treating mature trees

4.5 Economic Value Drivers

• Increased production: 10–30% yield improvements are common as soil health

• Consistency: Reduced year-to-year variability creates predictable revenue
• Quality improvements: Better fruit/nut quality commands premium pricing
• Risk reduction: Healthy soils buffer against weather stress and disease

SOIL HEALTH IMPROVEMENTS:

• Water infiltration: 50%+ improvement reduces runoff and increases retention
• Nutrient retention: Healthy soils hold nutrients, reducing leaching and waste
• Biological activity: Active soil biology cycles nutrients more efficiently

FERTILIZER REDUCTION (COST SAVINGS):

• Year 2–3: 20–30% reduction in synthetic fertilizer needs as biology takes over
• Year 4+: 30–40% reduction possible as soil becomes self-sustaining
• Cost savings: $50–$200+ per acre annually in reduced fertilizer costs

LONG-TERM ROI:

• Year 1: Foundation investment (higher cost, building the system)
• Years 2–3: Maintenance phase (reduced costs, increasing benefits)
• Years 4+: Compound returns (lower costs, higher yields, better quality)
• 5-year ROI: Typically 2–4x return on investment when all factors considered
• Extended lifespan: 25–75+ year productive lifespan vs. 15–20 years = 
  generational value

RISK MITIGATION:

• Drought resilience: Better water efficiency reduces irrigation dependency
• Disease resistance: Healthy soils and trees resist pathogens naturally
• Market volatility: Quality improvements provide pricing flexibility
• Regulatory compliance: Regenerative practices align with sustainability 
  requirements
• Succession planning: Healthy orchards become valuable legacy assets

ECONOMIC CALCULATION FRAMEWORK:

When presenting economic value, use this framework:

1. Current State Baseline
   • Current yield (tons/acre or lbs/tree)
   • Current fertilizer costs per acre
   • Current water usage and costs
   • Current quality metrics and pricing

2. Projected Improvements (Year 3–5)
   • Yield increase: 10–30% (conservative estimate)
   • Fertilizer reduction: 20–40% (as biology takes over)
   • Water efficiency: 20–40% reduction in irrigation needs
   • Quality premium: 5–15% price improvement (if applicable)

3. Investment vs. Return
   • Program cost per acre (Year 1, Year 2–3, Year 4+)
   • Cost savings: Fertilizer + water reduction
   • Revenue increase: Yield lift × price
   • Net improvement: Revenue increase + cost savings - program cost
   • ROI timeline: When does cumulative benefit exceed cumulative cost?

4. Long-Term Value
   • Extended orchard lifespan: 10–50+ additional productive years
   • Legacy value: Asset appreciation and generational transfer
   • Risk reduction: Insurance value of resilient operation

5. CRM INTEGRATION - COMPLETE WORKFLOW
================================================================================

Effective CRM integration is the backbone of successful orchard, vineyard, and 
specialty crop program execution. This section provides a quick overview of the 
complete workflow for capturing, segmenting, engaging, and converting leads 
through every stage of the sales pipeline. All activities are tracked in the 
CRM to ensure visibility, accountability, and data-driven decision-making.

Note: Detailed sales tactics, scripts, communication templates, and step-by-step 
procedures are covered in subsequent sections (Outreach Strategy & Tactics, Lead 
Nurturing Strategy, Sales Process). This section focuses on the CRM workflow 
structure and automation framework to provide context for the detailed processes 
that follow.

5.1 Workflow Overview
================================================================================

The complete CRM workflow follows this progression:

Lead Capture → Segmentation → Smart Calling (Decision Maker ID) → Farm Visits → 
Deals & Conversions → Reporting, Tracking & Analytics

Each stage is supported by automated workflows that ensure consistent execution 
and prevent leads from falling through the cracks. The CRM serves as the central 
hub for all sales activities, providing real-time visibility into pipeline health 
and team performance.

5.2 Lead Capture & Entry
================================================================================

Lead capture is the entry point for all prospects entering the SSW system. 
Leads are captured through multiple channels: Google Maps scraping, trade shows, 
website forms, referrals, and cold outreach responses. Every lead must be 
properly entered into the CRM with complete information (contact details, farm 
information, crop type, acreage, geographic data) to enable effective 
segmentation, automation, and follow-up.

Upon entry, the system automatically assigns leads to representatives, applies 
segmentation tags, calculates initial lead scores, and triggers welcome email 
sequences. Entry deadline: December 15, 2024 for initial lead list.

5.3 Segmentation & Tagging
================================================================================

Leads are segmented primarily by crop type to ensure the right product message 
(Pomona, Seriokai's Secret, or Bucchas) reaches the right prospect. Additional 
segmentation dimensions include geographic zones, operation size, engagement 
level, sales stage, and decision timeline. Automatic segmentation workflows 
apply tags upon entry and update dynamically as leads progress through the 
pipeline.

5.4 Smart Calling & Decision Maker ID
================================================================================

Smart calling prioritizes outreach based on engagement data (email opens, 
clicks, replies) and focuses on identifying and connecting with decision makers. 
Call lists are generated from CRM engagement metrics, with priority given to 
hot leads who haven't been called recently. All call outcomes are logged in CRM 
with detailed notes, and automated follow-up tasks are created based on results.

5.5 Automations & Lead Nurturing
================================================================================

Automation reduces manual work and ensures consistency. Core automations include: 
(1) Lead nurturing sequences that send crop-specific value content every 14 days, 
(2) Stage-based follow-up sequences (post-visit, post-sample, post-proposal), 
(3) Automatic task creation based on activities, (4) Lead scoring that updates 
based on engagement, and (5) Stage progression automation that moves leads 
through the pipeline based on activity and scores.

5.6 Farm Visits & Documentation
================================================================================

Farm visits are scheduled monthly during dedicated field visit weeks, with route 
optimization to maximize efficiency. Qualified leads (score 30+, decision maker 
identified) are scheduled for visits. All visit activities, photos, notes, and 
recommendations are logged in CRM within 24 hours. Automated thank-you emails 
and follow-up sequences are triggered post-visit.

5.7 Deals & Conversions
================================================================================

Deals are created when qualified leads show serious interest and move through 
pipeline stages: Opportunity Identified → Proposal Sent → Proposal Under Review 
→ Negotiating → Contract Sent → Closed-Won/Closed-Lost. Deal values are 
calculated based on acreage, blend type, and application rates. Probability 
tracking provides weighted pipeline value. All deal activities are logged in CRM 
for complete visibility.

5.8 Reporting, Tracking & Analytics
================================================================================

Essential tracking includes email metrics (sent, opened, clicked, replied), call 
tracking (outcomes, connection rates), visit tracking (scheduled, completed, 
conversion rates), and deal tracking (proposals, stages, win/loss). Weekly and 
monthly dashboards provide pipeline health, activity metrics, and revenue 
projections. All data exports support route planning and targeted outreach.

7. LEAD NURTURING STRATEGY
================================================================================

Lead nurturing is the systematic process of building relationships with prospects 
through consistent, value-driven communication. This strategy transforms cold 
leads into warm prospects and ultimately into engaged customers by providing 
education, establishing credibility, and maintaining top-of-mind awareness.

The nurturing strategy combines automated email sequences with personalized 
touchpoints, creating a "I am automation" approach where automation handles 
routine communication while representatives focus on high-value personal 
interactions.

KEY PRINCIPLES:

• Value First: Every communication must provide genuine value before asking for anything
• Consistency: Regular touchpoints maintain relationship momentum
• Personalization: Automation feels personal through segmentation and customization
• Education-Focused: Build trust through knowledge sharing, not selling
• Multi-Channel: Email automation supplemented with personal calls, texts, and visits
• Progressive Engagement: Move from awareness to interest to action systematically

STRATEGIC FOUNDATION:

This nurturing strategy is built on proven methodologies from:
• Hook Point (Brendan Kane): Attention-grabbing messaging that cuts through noise
• Smart Calling (Art Sobczak): Strategic, value-focused communication
• SPIN Selling (Neil Rackham): Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-payoff questioning
• Made to Stick (Chip & Dan Heath): Memorable, valuable content that resonates
• Stories That Stick (Kindra Hall): Storytelling that builds connection and credibility

7.1 5-Email Nurture Series
================================================================================

The 5-Email Nurture Series is an automated sequence that runs every 14 days, 
delivering value-focused content to prospects who have been contacted but haven't 
yet engaged or converted. This series builds trust, educates prospects, and 
positions SSW as a knowledgeable partner.

SERIES OVERVIEW:

• Frequency: One email every 14 days (2 weeks)
• Total Duration: 10 weeks (70 days)
• Goal: Progressive education and relationship building
• Automation: Fully automated in CRM with personalization tokens
• Exit Triggers: Engagement (reply, click, call) moves prospect to personal follow-up

EMAIL 1: THE HOOK POINT (Day 0 - Initial Contact)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

OBJECTIVE: Capture attention and establish relevance

SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS:
• "[Crop Type] Farmers: One Soil Health Insight That Changes Everything"
• "Why [Farm Name]'s [Crop] Operation Needs This Soil Strategy"
• "The [Crop] Challenge Most Farmers Don't See Coming"

OPENING HOOK (First 2 sentences):
• Reference their specific crop type and geographic region
• Pose a thought-provoking question or share a surprising insight
• Example: "If you're growing [crop] in [region], you're likely facing [specific challenge]. 
  Most farmers don't realize this challenge is actually a soil health opportunity."

BODY STRUCTURE:
• Problem identification (their challenge)
• Insight or education (valuable information)
• Soft value proposition (how SSW addresses this)
• No hard sell - invitation to learn more

CALL-TO-ACTION:
• "Reply with your biggest soil challenge - I'll send you a free resource"
• "Click here to download our [Crop] Soil Health Guide"
• "Would a 15-minute call make sense to discuss your operation?"

EMAIL 2: EDUCATIONAL VALUE (Day 14)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

OBJECTIVE: Provide actionable education that demonstrates expertise

SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS:
• "The [Crop] Soil Health Factor Most Farmers Overlook"
• "3 Soil Health Insights for [Crop] Operations Like Yours"
• "Why [Crop] Quality Starts Below the Surface"

CONTENT FOCUS:
• Deep dive into one specific soil health topic relevant to their crop
• Share case study or example (anonymized if needed)
• Provide actionable insights they can implement
• Reference SSW methodology without heavy selling

VALUE DELIVERY:
• Educational content that positions SSW as expert
• Practical tips they can use immediately
• Builds credibility through knowledge sharing

CALL-TO-ACTION:
• "Want to see how this applies to your specific operation?"
• "Reply with questions - happy to help"
• "Download our free [Crop] Protocol Guide"

EMAIL 3: SOCIAL PROOF & STORIES (Day 28)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

OBJECTIVE: Build credibility through success stories and social proof

SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS:
• "How [Similar Farm] Increased [Crop] Quality by [X]%"
• "The [Crop] Farmer Who Cut Fertilizer Costs by [X]%"
• "Real Results: [Crop] Operations Using Regenerative Practices"

CONTENT FOCUS:
• Success story from similar operation (same crop type, similar size)
• Specific results and outcomes (yield, quality, cost savings)
• Challenges overcome and solutions implemented
• Testimonial or quote from satisfied customer

STORYTELLING ELEMENTS:
• Problem → Solution → Results structure
• Specific details that resonate with their situation
• Authentic, relatable narrative

CALL-TO-ACTION:
• "Want to see if this approach could work for [Farm Name]?"
• "Schedule a 20-minute call to discuss your situation"
• "Reply to learn more about our [Crop] programs"

EMAIL 4: PROBLEM-SOLUTION FRAMING (Day 42)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

OBJECTIVE: Connect their challenges to SSW solutions through SPIN methodology

SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS:
• "The Hidden Cost of [Common Challenge] in [Crop] Operations"
• "Why [Crop] Farmers Are Switching to Regenerative Practices"
• "The [Crop] Challenge That's Costing You More Than You Think"

CONTENT STRUCTURE (SPIN Framework):
• SITUATION: Acknowledge their current situation/challenges
• PROBLEM: Identify specific problems they're likely facing
• IMPLICATION: Show the cost/impact of not addressing these problems
• NEED-PAYOFF: Present how addressing these creates value

VALUE PROPOSITION:
• Connect problems to solutions
• Quantify the cost of inaction
• Present SSW approach as logical solution

CALL-TO-ACTION:
• "Want to see how much [specific problem] is costing your operation?"
• "Let's discuss your [crop] challenges - no pressure, just insights"
• "Schedule a free soil health assessment"

EMAIL 5: INVITATION TO ENGAGE (Day 56)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

OBJECTIVE: Create urgency and invite direct engagement

SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS:
• "Final Thought: Why [Crop] Operations Like Yours Need This Now"
• "One Last Thing About [Farm Name]'s Soil Health"
• "Before You Delete This: One [Crop] Insight Worth Your Time"

CONTENT FOCUS:
• Recap key value delivered in previous emails
• Reinforce main value proposition
• Create gentle urgency (seasonal timing, opportunity cost)
• Personal invitation to connect

TONE:
• Conversational and personal
• Respectful of their time
• Clear but not pushy

CALL-TO-ACTION:
• "Ready to discuss how this applies to [Farm Name]?"
• "Let's schedule a 15-minute call - I'll bring specific insights for your operation"
• "Reply 'yes' if you'd like to learn more - no obligation"

POST-SERIES AUTOMATION:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

After Email 5, prospects enter one of three paths:

1. ENGAGED PROSPECTS (replied, clicked, called):
   • Moved to personal follow-up queue
   • Representative receives notification
   • Personal outreach scheduled within 48 hours

2. WARM BUT NOT ENGAGED:
   • Enter monthly newsletter sequence
   • Continue receiving value content
   • Periodic re-engagement campaigns

3. COLD/UNRESPONSIVE:
   • Moved to quarterly re-engagement campaigns
   • Lower priority in CRM
   • Can be re-activated with new hook point

Day 14: Email 2 sent automatically

7.5 Education → Credibility → Proposal Flow
================================================================================

The nurturing strategy follows a progressive flow that moves prospects from 
awareness to action through education, credibility building, and ultimately 
proposal delivery. This flow ensures prospects are well-informed and ready 
before receiving a formal proposal.

PROGRESSIVE ENGAGEMENT FLOW:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STAGE 1: EDUCATION (Weeks 1-4)
• Goal: Provide value and build awareness
• Tactics: Hook point email, educational content, newsletters
• Outcome: Prospect recognizes SSW as knowledgeable resource

STAGE 2: CREDIBILITY (Weeks 5-8)
• Goal: Build trust through social proof and expertise
• Tactics: Case studies, success stories, personal interactions
• Outcome: Prospect trusts SSW as credible partner

STAGE 3: ENGAGEMENT (Weeks 9-12)
• Goal: Create interest and desire to learn more
• Tactics: Discovery calls, site visits, samples, personal follow-ups
• Outcome: Prospect actively engages and wants to explore solutions

STAGE 4: PROPOSAL (Weeks 13+)
• Goal: Present formal solution and close
• Tactics: Customized proposal, ROI calculations, contract discussion
• Outcome: Prospect becomes customer

EDUCATION PHASE TACTICS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

• Deliver valuable content consistently
• Answer questions thoroughly
• Provide resources and guides
• Share insights and best practices
• Position as educator, not seller

CREDIBILITY PHASE TACTICS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

• Share success stories and case studies
• Provide testimonials and references
• Demonstrate expertise through content
• Show results and outcomes
• Build personal relationship

ENGAGEMENT PHASE TACTICS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

• Conduct discovery conversations
• Schedule site visits
• Provide samples and trials
• Personal follow-up calls
• Address specific challenges

PROPOSAL PHASE TACTICS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

• Customize proposal to their situation
• Present ROI calculations
• Address objections proactively
• Provide clear next steps
• Facilitate decision-making

TRANSITION SIGNALS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Move to next stage when:

EDUCATION → CREDIBILITY:
• Prospect engages with content
• Prospect asks questions
• Prospect shows interest in learning more

CREDIBILITY → ENGAGEMENT:
• Prospect requests information
• Prospect agrees to call or meeting
• Prospect expresses specific challenges

ENGAGEMENT → PROPOSAL:
• Prospect requests proposal
• Prospect is qualified (acreage, budget, authority)
• Prospect shows buying signals

PROPOSAL → CLOSE:
• Prospect reviews proposal
• Prospect asks closing questions
• Prospect is ready to decide

CRM TRACKING:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Each stage is tracked in CRM:

• Stage assignment based on engagement
• Automated workflows for each stage
• Representative tasks and follow-ups
• Progress metrics and reporting

MEASUREMENT:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Track progression through stages:

• Time in each stage
• Conversion rates between stages
• Drop-off points
• Average time to proposal
• Average time to close

8. SALES PROCESS
================================================================================

The sales process transforms qualified leads into customers through a systematic approach that builds trust, demonstrates value, and addresses concerns. This section outlines each step from initial qualification through contract execution.

8.1 QUALIFICATION STEPS & CRITERIA
================================================================================

QUALIFICATION FRAMEWORK (BANT + Fit):

BUDGET:
• Can they afford the program? (Estimate: $X per acre based on crop type and acreage)
• Current fertilizer/input spending range
• Budget decision-making authority
• Payment terms flexibility
• Multi-year commitment potential

AUTHORITY:
• Are they the decision-maker or can they influence the decision?
• Who else is involved in the decision? (Owner, farm manager, consultant, board)
• Decision-making process and timeline
• Approval requirements and stakeholders

NEED:
• Do they have pain points we can solve?
  - Soil health challenges (compaction, low OM, poor structure)
  - Water efficiency issues
  - Fertilizer cost concerns
  - Yield or quality problems
  - Nutrient deficiencies
• Current program satisfaction level
• Desire to improve or change current approach

TIMELINE:
• When would they consider making a change?
• Seasonal timing considerations
• Budget cycle and planning periods
• Urgency indicators (current problems, upcoming decisions)

FIT:
• Crop type suitability (pistachio, pome, stone fruit, avocado, citrus, vineyard)
• Acreage size (minimum viable: 20+ acres, ideal: 50+ acres)
• Geographic accessibility (within 300-mile radius)
• Operation type and management style
• Alignment with SSW regenerative approach

QUALIFICATION SCORING:
• High Priority (Score 8-10): Meets all BANT criteria + strong fit
• Medium Priority (Score 5-7): Meets most criteria, some gaps
• Low Priority (Score 1-4): Missing key criteria, poor fit

DISQUALIFICATION CRITERIA:
• No budget or authority
• Unrealistic timeline (beyond 12 months)
• Poor crop/geographic fit
• Not interested in regenerative approach
• Current program satisfaction with no desire to change

8.2 SITE VISIT SCHEDULING & PREPARATION
================================================================================

SCHEDULING PROCESS:
• Schedule site visits during monthly field visit weeks
• Use CRM calendar integration for scheduling
• Confirm visit 48 hours in advance
• Send preparation email with agenda and what to expect
• Coordinate with farm manager/owner availability
• Group visits geographically for route efficiency

PRE-VISIT PREPARATION:
• Review CRM profile and all previous interactions

• Bring relevant case studies or examples
• Prepare sample products (9 lb trial bags)

VISIT AGENDA:
1. Farm tour and visual assessment (15-20 minutes)
2. Discovery conversation (20-30 minutes)
3. Soil observation and discussion (10-15 minutes)
4. Value proposition discussion (15-20 minutes)
5. Next steps and follow-up plan (5-10 minutes)

ON-SITE ASSESSMENT CHECKLIST:
• Visual crop health observations
• Soil structure and compaction signs
• Irrigation system type and condition
• Current fertilizer/amendment storage
• Equipment availability
• Access and logistics considerations
• Photos: crop condition, soil, infrastructure
• Notes: specific challenges observed, farmer concerns

POST-VISIT DOCUMENTATION:
• Log visit in CRM immediately (same day)
• Upload photos to CRM record
• Document key findings and observations
• Record pain points and opportunities
• Note decision-maker insights
• Update qualification score
• Set follow-up tasks and timeline
• Move to appropriate pipeline stage

8.4 PRICING LOGIC & CALCULATIONS
================================================================================

PRICING STRUCTURE:

PER-ACRE PRICING (Primary Method):
• Base rate per acre for product blend
• Varies by crop type and application rate
• Example: $X per acre for Pomona blend
• Minimum acreage requirements (typically 20+ acres)

PER-TREE PRICING (Alternative):
• Used for smaller operations or specific crops
• Based on tree count and spacing
• Useful for high-value crops with lower tree density

VOLUME DISCOUNTS:
• Tier 1: 20-50 acres (standard pricing)
• Tier 2: 51-100 acres (5% discount)
• Tier 3: 101-200 acres (10% discount)
• Tier 4: 200+ acres (15% discount)

MULTI-YEAR COMMITMENTS:
• Year 1: Standard pricing
• Year 2: 5% discount for commitment
• Year 3+: 10% discount for extended commitment
• Lock in pricing for contract duration

PAYMENT TERMS:
• Standard: Net 30 days
• Options: Net 15 (2% discount), Net 60 (standard)
• Seasonal payment plans available
• Pre-payment discounts (5% for full payment upfront)

ROI CALCULATION FRAMEWORK:

CURRENT COSTS:
• Annual fertilizer spending per acre
• Amendment costs
• Application costs
• Total current input investment

PROGRAM INVESTMENT:
• Product costs per acre
• Application/service fees
• Total program cost

EXPECTED SAVINGS:
• Fertilizer reduction (typically 20-40%)
• Water efficiency improvements
• Reduced application frequency

EXPECTED GAINS:
• Yield improvement value (5-15% typical)
• Quality premium potential
• Long-term soil health value

NET ROI:
• (Savings + Gains) - Program Investment
• Payback period calculation
• Multi-year cumulative ROI

PRICING NEGOTIATION GUIDELINES:
• Start with standard pricing
• Use volume and commitment discounts as negotiation tools

• Focus on value and ROI, not just price
• Trial programs at standard pricing (no discount on trials)

8.5 CLOSING STEPS & CONTRACT EXECUTION
================================================================================

CLOSING FRAMEWORK (Based on "Never Split the Difference" & "Start with No"):

NEGOTIATION PRINCIPLES:

1. ACTIVE LISTENING & LABELING:
   • Listen for underlying concerns, not just stated objections
   • Use labeling: "It sounds like...", "It seems like...", "I'm hearing that..."
   • Validate their perspective before responding
   • Example: "It sounds like you're concerned about the upfront investment"

2. CALIBRATED QUESTIONS:
   • Ask "How" and "What" questions to understand their position
   • Avoid "Why" questions (can sound accusatory)
   • Examples:
     - "How would this program need to work for you to move forward?"
     - "What would need to happen for this to make sense?"
     - "How can we structure this to address your concerns?"

3. AVOID SPLITTING THE DIFFERENCE:
   • Don't automatically meet in the middle
   • Understand what they really need vs. what they're asking for
   • Trade value, not just price
   • Get something in return for any concessions

4. START WITH "NO":
   • "No" is not rejection, it's the start of a conversation
   • "No" means "not right now" or "not in this way"
   • Use "No" to uncover real objections
   • Example: "If the answer was no, what would need to change?"

5. ANCHORING & FRAMING:
   • Anchor with value and ROI, not price
   • Frame in terms of their goals and outcomes
   • Use loss aversion: "What happens if you don't address these soil issues?"
   • Compare to current costs, not to zero

CLOSING PROCESS:

STEP 1: IDENTIFY READINESS SIGNALS:
• Asking specific questions about implementation
• Discussing timeline and start dates
• Talking about budget or payment terms
• Requesting references or case studies
• Comparing to other options
• Involving other decision-makers in conversation

STEP 2: HANDLE FINAL OBJECTIONS:
• Listen completely before responding
• Label the concern: "It sounds like you're worried about..."
• Ask calibrated questions to understand the real issue
• Address with specific information or alternatives
• Confirm understanding: "Does that address your concern?"

COMMON FINAL OBJECTIONS & RESPONSES:

"We need to think about it":
• "I understand. What specifically do you need to think about?"
• "What information would help you make a decision?"
• "What concerns do you have that we haven't addressed?"

"The price is too high":
• "I understand price is a consideration. What would make this worth the investment?"
• "Compared to your current fertilizer costs, how does this compare?"
• "What ROI would you need to see to make this worthwhile?"

"We want to try a smaller area first":
• "That makes sense. What size trial would you be comfortable with?"
• "How would we measure success in the trial?"
• "What would need to happen in the trial for you to expand?"

"We need to talk to our consultant/partner":
• "Absolutely. Who else is involved in this decision?"
• "What questions do you think they'll have?"
• "Would it help if I spoke with them directly?"

STEP 3: PROPOSE NEXT STEP:
• Be specific: "I'd like to propose we move forward with..."
• Set timeline: "If we can get started by [date], we can..."
• Create urgency (if genuine): "To hit the optimal application window..."
• Offer trial option: "Would you like to start with a trial on X acres?"

STEP 4: HANDLE NEGOTIATION:
• If they ask for discount: "I understand. What would make this work for you?"
• Trade value: "If you commit to [larger acreage/multi-year], I can..."
• Never give something without getting something
• Focus on total value, not just price

STEP 5: CLOSE THE DEAL:
• Summarize agreement: "So we're agreed on..."
• Confirm details: pricing, acreage, timeline, terms
• Set contract execution timeline
• Schedule next steps: contract delivery, implementation planning

CONTRACT EXECUTION:

CONTRACT COMPONENTS:
• Farm/company information
• Program details (products, rates, acreage)
• Pricing and payment terms
• Implementation timeline and schedule
• Delivery and logistics
• Terms and conditions
• Signatures and dates

CONTRACT DELIVERY:
• Send contract within 24 hours of verbal agreement
• Follow up within 48 hours if not returned
• Offer to review contract together (call or in-person)
• Address any questions or concerns promptly

POST-CONTRACT:
• Welcome email/call
• Implementation planning meeting
• Schedule soil test (if not done)
• Coordinate delivery and application
• Set up monitoring and follow-up schedule
• Move to "Customer" stage in CRM

DEAL STALLED? RE-ENGAGEMENT STRATEGY:
• If no response after proposal: Wait 7 days, then send value content
• If no response after contract: Wait 5 days, then call with calibrated question
• If objection raised: Use labeling and calibrated questions
• If timeline extended: Stay engaged with value content, check in monthly
• If lost to competitor: Ask what would have made the difference (learning opportunity)
